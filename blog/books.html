Why I Started Reading

Update – 08/04/2025: A short reflection on how I got into reading and the books that stuck with me.

Intro

I never used to read. Not really. School killed the joy of it for me early on—mandatory novels, boring interpretations, endless overanalysis. For years, books were just something I had to “get through.” Until something shifted.

At some point, I started craving depth. I was tired of endless feeds, shallow content, clickbait summaries. I wanted something that took time to unfold. Something that didn’t scream for attention but stayed with me. That’s when I picked up a book again—voluntarily.

Why I Started Reading

Reading slowed me down in the best way. It gave me space to think. Unlike videos or articles, a book doesn’t rush you. You move at your own pace. And in that space, I found clarity. It also helped me focus longer, reflect deeper, and stay offline more often.

I also realized that good books are like long conversations with smart people. You’re not just entertained—you’re learning how someone else thinks, sees, lives. That became addictive in a way scrolling never was.

My Favorite Books (So Far)

Here are some of the books that hit hardest or stayed with me:

  • The War of Art – Steven Pressfield
    About resistance, creativity, and the discipline of showing up. Brutally honest and motivating.
  • Steal Like an Artist – Austin Kleon
    Short, practical, and fun. A creative mindset guide disguised as a tiny book.
  • Digital Minimalism – Cal Newport
    Helped me rethink my phone, habits, and attention. Gave structure to something I already felt.
  • Deep Work – Cal Newport
    A mindset book on focus and value in a distracted world. Feels especially relevant today.
  • Ikigai – Héctor García & Francesc Miralles
    Gentle and simple. A Japanese concept of purpose, health, and living with intention.

Final Thoughts

Reading gave me something I didn’t even realize I was missing: mental stillness. The kind of focus you don’t get from your phone or your laptop. Just one sentence at a time, pulling you in. And slowly, you feel yourself returning to the world with more clarity.

I still don’t read every day. But when I do, it feels like time well spent. And in a busy, noisy life—that’s rare.

— Maico Minne. Written 08/04/2025
