The Focused Life

Update – 04/08/2025: Originally written as a blog post, but I’ve decided to explore this topic further in a future book.

Intro

Growing up as part of Gen-Z in the Western world, the pressure to live a fully digital life is intense. We’re expected to share every moment on Instagram, get our news from YouTube, be entertained on TikTok, buy everything on Amazon, and spend hours immersed in games. It’s how most people live—and we rarely question it. When everyone around you is online, it’s almost taboo not to follow.

We often act without thinking. We're slaves to impulse, driven by short-term pleasures and the next dopamine rush—the new product, the next release, the fleeting satisfaction. And yet, we still feel empty. This cycle of desire and disappointment defines the lives of so many in my generation. Yes, to desire is human. But we’ve started confusing desire with value—and that’s dangerous.

Why

Gen-Z is called the distracted generation, the connected generation, the indulgent generation—and this generation will soon run the world. Being part of it, I think I have a unique perspective. I want to reflect honestly on how we got here, and what the consequences might be if we don’t change.

Why do we live like this? Why are we expected to? How did it get to this point? These questions aren’t easy—and sometimes even thinking about them gives me anxiety. But that’s exactly why we need to ask them. If we don’t understand our behaviors, how can we expect to grow or live better?

Value

Humans have always chased meaning. But my generation—comfortable, shielded, and overstimulated—struggles with finding any. We've been spared hardship, and as a result, even the smallest discomfort feels unbearable. What sets us apart is our constant access to distraction. It's never been easier to escape reality.

As traditional belief systems fade, nihilism quietly takes their place. Without a sense of higher purpose, it's hard to know what to aim for. We become drifters, chasing whatever feels good in the moment, because we don’t know what else to pursue. And in a world that tells us life has no intrinsic meaning—what’s left to believe in?

The Search — Finding a Balanced Lifestyle

Is it still possible to live intentionally in a hyperconnected world? Can we choose depth over distraction? It feels like we’re caught between two extremes: becoming digital hermits or fully surrendering to the chaos. But maybe there’s a middle path. Maybe we don’t have to abandon tech—but we can choose how it fits into our lives.

A Wholesome Existence

A focused life isn’t about being perfect. It’s about being present. It’s about choosing clarity over noise, connection over performance, and growth over indulgence. It’s hard—but it’s worth it.

Concluding Thoughts — Who Is This For?

This reflection isn’t meant to preach. I still struggle with distractions every day. But I believe there’s a growing number of us—especially in Gen-Z—who want more. Who are tired of living on autopilot. If you’re one of them, this is for you. Let’s choose focus. Let’s choose meaning.

— Maico Minne. Written 04/08/2025
